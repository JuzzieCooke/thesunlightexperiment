Coluracetam

Ampakine

Coluracetam is a new nootropic compound developed by Mitsubishi Tanabe Pharma Corporation as a treatment for Alzheimer's disease. It has a unique ability to increasing vision by making colours more vibrant to those using it. It works in a similar way to the other racetams, where it increases choline uptake to improve neurotransmitter functioning through acetylcholine. It's thought to be useful for Alzheimer's where the HACU (high affinity Choline uptake) function is impaired.

Flourenol

Alcohol

Researchers at the biopharmaceutical company Cephalon have been working on Flourenol (Hydrafinil) for the last several years. Its effects are very similar to Modafinil. In fact, it's one of the active metabolites of modafinil. The effects are shorter in duration than Modafninil, but considered to be roughly 40% stronger.

Flourenol is also showing usefulness as a way to speed the recovery of amphetamine addiction.

The mechanism of action is still not well understood, but is thought to be through dopamine reuptake inhibition, and 5-HT6 receptor antagonism.

Ampakine

PRL-8-53

Ampakine

PRL-8-53 is a nootropic derived from phenylmethylamine and benzoic acid. Its chemical name is Methyl 3-[2-[benzyl(methyl)amino]ethyl]benzoate. It was actually invented in the 1970's by professor Nikolaus Hansl at Creighton University. Despite its proven effectiveness and safety, PRL-8-53 never received further research, which was blamed on a lack of funding for Nikolaus Hansl after he left the university during a lawsuit. he has since passed away.
